On '100%' Mormonism

Posted on Jun 8, 2022 One min read

In the set of all people who are or have ever been Mormons, I think the number of people who believe 100% of everything church leaders have ever taught is effectively zero. I don’t claim this is indicative of hypocrisy or unrest in the church, just that it opposes the way a lot of Mormons see themselves.

There is a subtype of Mormon that plays the role of someone who believes all Church teachings without reservation—a “100 percenter,” to coin a phrase. They often occupy leadership roles in their congregation, appear to be happy in their church participation, and voluntarily contribute time and effort beyond what’s required. While they’d be offended at an accusation of “blind faith,” it’s indeed what they have. Anytime someone defends a belief or practice they can’t fully explain, that’s blind faith—even if they consider themselves as having ample reason to trust its source.
