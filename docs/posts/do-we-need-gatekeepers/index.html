Do we need gatekeepers?

On supply, demand, and the wizard complex

Posted on May 1, 2018 One min read

Writing code is the nearest you’ll ever get to doing actual magic.

Think about the last fantasy book you read. Witches and wizards follow a time-honored pattern: they say a couple of special words and wave their hands and then incredible things happen. Harry Potter says lumos. Doctor Strange says by the Eye of Agamotto. Gandalf says you shall not pass. Kenobi says these aren’t the droids you’re looking for. And programmers say if (input % 3 == 0) log("fizz"). If you know the words to say, you can make anything happen in a virtual world.

It’s satisfying to know this art and perform it well, and especially satisfying to do so in front of people who don’t understand what you’re doing.
