Litter, an allegory for social change

Posted on Nov 11, 2023 3 mins read

Cityville (population one million) has a litter problem. You can’t walk ten feet without skirting around piles of crushed soda cans, cigarette butts, cardboard, and plastic bags. It’s part of the city’s reputation at this point. It’s dragging down property values and everything.

A group of students at the local campus decides it’s up to them to solve the problem. If they don’t do it, who will? However, they also need to get a research paper out of it, so they divide the city into sectors and each student makes a plan for their sector.

The Politician

The first student mounts a campaign for the upcoming city council election. Their platform is fixing the litter problem. They probably won’t win, seeing as they don’t have a history in politics, but simply running on litter mitigation makes it an election issue, which will force the other candidates to respond.

The Founder

The second student forms a nonprofit, recruits some volunteers, distributes branded T-shirts, and starts picking up litter in high-traffic areas. When donations start rolling in, they use the money (with approval from city officials) to place more garbage cans near sidewalks and public-use areas.

The Advertiser

The third student partners with local businesses to run a “Keep Our Streets Clean” ad campaign. They post flyers, hand out brochures, set up a booth at the farmer’s market, and even rent a billboard. The campaign generates a lot of buzz, so they get some free airtime on the evening news.

The Inventor

The fourth student designs a 3D-printable device called the “Clamtrap” that can effortlessly bag and tie 8 cubic feet of trash. They license the design as public domain and put up notices in local maker spaces and 3D print shops to let people know about it. They even get a few shops to agree to sell the device at cost.

The Protester

The fifth student organizes protests against city leadership. There are handmade signs, call-and-response chants, social media groups, all that jazz. Plenty of people are already fired up about the issue, so it’s no trouble staffing a picket line in front of the city offices twice a day.

The Screamer

The sixth student hangs out near busy streets and public parks with a pair of binoculars. When they see someone littering, they run up to them and scream insults and obscenities. They also take the offender’s picture and post it on social media with the caption “SHAME!!!!” On days when the Screamer can’t be bothered to go outside, they snoop around on Instagram to find people who don’t express sufficiently anti-litter stances, or who are silent on the issue altogether, and send them death threats.

You could probably come up with ten or fifteen more ideas, but of these six, which do you think is most effective?

If you’re asking me, I honestly don’t know. But can we all agree it’s not the Screamer? That student is wasting their time.

Some people really seem to think the Screamer has it all figured out. That worries me.
