Self

On ugliness

And the human form

Posted on May 2, 2022 One min read

No one is ugly. I’m not trying to be a motivational meme, I’m inspecting the meaning of is.

A person cannot be ugly because ugly is not a thing that it is possible to be. Ugliness is not a property of living things. A wolf alone in a void, for example, is neither ugly nor beautiful. It may be small, large, brown, white, dappled, diabetic, hungry, tired, alive, or dead. These things can be observed without any judgment or humanity. They can be true even when no one is present to observe them. “Beauty is in the eye of the beholder,” the saying goes, and without a beholder the whole question is moot.

To say whether the wolf is ugly, you must draw on a deep well of experiences, values, and biases. Maybe it looks different than other wolves you have seen. That might be sufficient for you to call it ugly. Maybe, against all odds, it has a clean, shiny fur coat. Those who value cleanliness may call that beautiful. Maybe it limps or has lost an eye. We may have harmful beliefs about disability ranging from unfamiliarity to hatred. We can parse those in an instant, subconsciously, when we judge whether something is ugly.
