Where is all the good software?

Posted on Oct 31, 2023 One min read

No one would fault you for assuming that the goals of a software company are:

  1. To find problems that customers have.
  2. To build software that solves those problems.
  3. To charge as much money for it as customers can be persuaded to pay.
  4. To minimize the cost and complexity of doing so.

But if you work backwards, determining a company’s goals based on its budget, activities, and internal incentives, you’d often find that these are nowhere to be seen. Many solo developers and small teams are fantastic at building useful and reliable software. But nearly every company with a board of directors is catering primarily to other goals, some of which are bizarre.

Following are some examples.

  1. To perform for an audience (private investors, the stock market, news media, each other)
  2. To keep salaries and incentives depressed
  3. To avoid any possibility of a lawsuit
  4. To hide organizational incompetence
  5. To advertise and hire
