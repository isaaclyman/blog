Why are some people bad?

Posted on Jun 20, 2022 One min read

Some people are bad, or at least more bad than good. There are potentially unlimited ways to define what bad is. But for the sake of discussion, let’s say a bad person is someone who wilfully and habitually harms others for selfish reasons. Harm is an expansive term encompassing not just physical harm but also verbal abuse, manipulation, theft, property damage, and refusal to assist when one could easily do so.

There is no uncomplicated, clear-cut line between good and evil. Context is important. Very few actions are always bad or always good. Most of us are bad sometimes, and badness is a spectrum that ranges from mild to severe. Still, there are some people we can confidently say are bad—say, those who commit mass violence or use prejudice to obtain political power.

It’s easy for a reasonable person to acknowledge that these people are bad. It’s much harder and less comfortable to discuss why they’re bad, why they do the things they do.
